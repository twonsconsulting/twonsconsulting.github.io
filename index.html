Before You Hire an AI Firm

Most firms calling themselves “AI companies” build wrappers around off-the-shelf language models. That’s fine for some problems — and completely wrong for others. Four questions that tell the difference.

1. What statistical methods do you use on complex data?

The hard problems are heterogeneous — high-dimensional spectra, sparse signals, sensor data, biometric patterns. They demand real methods: Bayesian inference, hierarchical models, principled uncertainty quantification. Anything less is guesswork dressed up as AI.

2. Can the methodology survive cross-examination?

Every score, ranking, or prediction should be traceable to a documented method. If it cannot be explained end-to-end, it cannot be defended.

3. What happens when the model disagrees with a domain expert?

Reconciling a model and an expert requires understanding both. Firms without domain credentials cannot do this honestly.

4. What would an audit record look like?

Regulators, courts, and investment committees do not accept “the AI said so.” Inputs, reasoning, and assumptions must be preserved and reviewable.

Two Ns gives defensible answers to all four. Here’s the work.
