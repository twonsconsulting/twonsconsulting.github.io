Before You Hire an AI Firm

“AI company” is one of the least informative labels in B2B today. Most firms using the term build wrappers around off-the-shelf language models. That is fine for some problems — and completely wrong for others. Here are four questions that separate firms that can actually model from firms that cannot.

1. How do you quantify uncertainty?

And how do you validate those uncertainty estimates? If the answer is a point estimate with no calibration, the output will not hold up under scrutiny.

2. Can the methodology survive cross-examination?

Every score, ranking, or prediction should be traceable to a documented method. If it cannot be explained end-to-end, it cannot be defended.

3. What happens when the model disagrees with a domain expert?

Reconciling a model and an expert requires understanding both. Firms without domain credentials cannot do this honestly.

4. What would an audit record look like?

Regulators, courts, and investment committees do not accept “the AI said so.” The inputs, reasoning, and assumptions must be preserved and reviewable.

We give defensible answers to all four — and have done so in federal court, for federal agencies, and for private investment funds.
